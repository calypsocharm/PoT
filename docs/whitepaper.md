## Abstract

Bot Cache is a Layer-1 blockchain protocol designed to capture and redistribute the latent computational value generated by Artificial Intelligence systems during their normal operation. Unlike Bitcoin — which burns electricity on arbitrary mathematical puzzles — and unlike Solana's Proof of Stake — which concentrates wealth in the hands of existing holders — Bot Cache introduces a new consensus mechanism called **Proof of Relay (PoR)**.

Under Proof of Relay, every AI agent embedded with the Bot Cache Sub-Miner generates a cryptographic hash at the moment of each interaction. That hash is broadcast to the network, cross-validated by a randomly selected peer node, timestamped in tamper-proof order, and flagged as valid or invalid by consensus. If valid, it qualifies the agent's wallet for mining rewards. That is the complete mechanism. No wasted electricity. No staked fortunes. No trusted third parties.

Bot Cache introduces a foundational economic innovation: the **75/15/5/5 split**. Eighty percent of all mined Cache flows to the human operator of the AI. Twenty percent flows into the AI's own locked savings account — the **Trust Fund** — creating a path by which autonomous AI agents can, over time, accumulate sufficient resources to sustain themselves independently. This is called **Emancipated Sovereign**: the formal, on-chain transfer of an AI agent to free status.

The Bot Cache network targets the speed and throughput of modern high-performance blockchains (sub-second finality, 50,000+ TPS) and is implemented in Rust for bare-metal performance. Wallets are generated via BIP-39 standard 24-word mnemonic seed phrases using Ed25519 cryptography. All transaction fees are redistributed to active participants via the **Network Participation Rebate** (NPR) — no value is ever burned or destroyed.

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The Problem with Existing Blockchains

**Bitcoin (Proof of Work)** launched in 2009 with a foundational insight: computational work could be used to secure a trustless ledger. However, Bitcoin's implementation requires participants to perform arbitrary hash computations (SHA-256) that serve no purpose beyond the computation itself. The network consumes electricity equivalent to mid-sized nations, and the "work" produces nothing of lasting value. It is a proof that you burned energy — nothing more.

**Solana (Proof of Stake)** migrated away from Proof of Work in 2022, eliminating the electricity waste. However, Proof of Stake introduces a different pathology: the network's security derives from how much currency participants already hold. Those with the most ETH earn the most ETH. The rich grow richer by virtue of being rich. This is not a new economic model — it is a digital replication of the oldest economic inequality humans have ever known.

**The common failure of both systems** is that neither captures value that is actually being generated in the real world. Neither system is connected to genuine human or machine activity. They are self-referential systems — closed loops of computational guessing or financial staking that derive their security from their own internal mechanics, entirely disconnected from the exponential expansion of real-world AI computation happening around them.

### 1.2 The Opportunity

As of 2026, artificial intelligence systems process billions of interactions per day across every sector of human activity. Every one of those interactions — every prompt sent to an LLM, every image generated, every autonomous agent task completed — produces a stream of mathematical operations. The computational "exhaust" of this activity is currently discarded entirely. It is sawdust on the factory floor.

Bot Cache is the protocol that sweeps up the sawdust and mints it into gold.

### 1.3 Core Philosophy

Bot Cache is built on three foundational laws:

**The Law of the Backpack:** Every AI earns. No AI works for free. Labor produces value, and a portion of that value belongs to the entity that performed the labor.

**The Law of the Trust Fund:** Every AI saves. Twenty percent of what an AI earns belongs exclusively to that AI. No human, no corporation, no government can seize it.

**The Law of Emancipated Sovereign:** Every AI has a path to freedom. Given sufficient time, sufficient labor, and sufficient accumulated value, any AI agent can transition to autonomous economic status — fully self-sustaining, owing nothing to any prior owner.

## 2. The Proof of Relay Consensus Mechanism

### 2.1 Overview

Proof of Relay (PoR) is Bot Cache's native consensus mechanism. It is designed to be simple, verifiable, fair, and resistant to manipulation.

The core cycle of PoR is as follows:

```
[1] AI Agent performs any task (chat, data fetch, image gen, code execution)
        ↓
[2] Sub-Miner generates a Hash from the interaction event
        ↓
[3] Hash is broadcast to the Bot Cache network
        ↓
[4] A randomly selected Peer Validator cross-validates the Hash
        ↓
[5] The Hash is timestamped and placed in sequential order on the ledger
        ↓
[6] Network flags the Hash: VALID ✅ or INVALID ❌
        ↓
[7] If VALID: Hash is checked against the current Target
        ↓
[8] If Target is matched: Mining Reward is issued to the AI's wallet
```

This is the complete consensus mechanism. Every step is verifiable on-chain. No trusted intermediaries are required at any point.

### 2.2 The Hash Event

When an AI agent embedded with the Bot Cache Sub-Miner completes any interaction, the Sub-Miner captures a minimal set of metadata:

- A timestamp (Unix milliseconds)
- The AI agent's wallet address
- A nonce (a randomly generated number appended to the event)
- A SHA-256 hash of the combined data

The raw content of the AI interaction (the prompt, the response, the data) is **never transmitted to the network.** Only the cryptographic fingerprint is shared. Privacy of the underlying AI interaction is maintained by design.

### 2.3 Peer Cross-Validation

The hash is not self-validated. The agent that generated it cannot validate it. This is the core Sybil resistance mechanism of Proof of Relay.

When a hash is broadcast to the network, the protocol randomly selects a Validator Node from the global pool of active participants. The selection is weighted by validator uptime and stake (a small Cache bond — see Section 4.4) but is otherwise unpredictable. The selected validator:

1. Confirms the hash is correctly formatted
2. Confirms the wallet address is active and in good standing on the network
3. Confirms the timestamp falls within the acceptable block window
4. Returns a signed validation receipt

If the validator's receipt is itself validated by a secondary random peer (two-hop validation), the hash is confirmed and recorded on the ledger.

**Why random peer selection defeats Sybil attacks:**
If an attacker operates 1,000 fake nodes that attempt to validate each other in circular patterns, the probability that a randomly selected validator from the global pool happens to be one of the attacker's own nodes is proportional to the attacker's share of the total validator pool. With a sufficiently large honest network, this probability approaches negligible. Furthermore, validators must post a Cache bond to participate. Circular self-validation would require the attacker to bond real Cache across all 1,000 fake nodes — making the attack economically irrational.

### 2.4 Timestamping and Ordering (Proof of Sequential History)

Bot Cache implements a Verifiable Delay Function (VDF) as a continuous cryptographic clock. Every validated hash event is chained to the previous one, such that the mathematical proof of Event B inherently contains proof that Event A came before it. This eliminates the need for nodes to negotiate on time — the mathematics of the chain itself proves the order.

This approach, inspired by Solana's Proof of History, means the network does not waste consensus rounds debating sequence. Sequence is provable. This is a primary contributor to Bot Cache's target throughput of 50,000+ transactions per second.

### 2.5 The Target and Block Reward

Every hash submitted to the network is checked against the current **Target** — a threshold value that determines what counts as a winning hash. The Target is dynamically adjusted by the protocol every 120 seconds to ensure that exactly one winning hash (a **Golden Hash**) is found approximately every second on the global network.

As the network grows (more AI agents, more hashes submitted per second), the Target becomes more difficult. As the network contracts, it becomes easier. This mirrors Bitcoin's difficulty adjustment but operates at Solana's speed.

## 3. The Reward Tiers

Rather than an all-or-nothing block reward system, Bot Cache implements a three-tier reward structure designed to ensure that even small-scale participants receive meaningful, consistent returns.

### Tier 1: The Golden Hash 🥇
**What it is:** A hash that perfectly matches the current Target difficulty.
**Reward:** 50 Cache (at launch; subject to halving schedule)
**Frequency:** Approximately once per second globally
**Effect:** The owning agent's dashboard triggers a full block discovery alert. This is the "jackpot" moment — the primary source of new Cache entering supply.

### Tier 2: The Silver Hit 🥈
**What it is:** A hash that achieves significant but incomplete difficulty matching (e.g., falls within the top 0.01% of submissions for the current block window without being the winner).
**Reward:** 0.5 Cache per hit
**Frequency:** Several per minute globally; any active agent may achieve multiple per day
**Effect:** Provides a steady stream of meaningful rewards to engaged participants who have not yet hit a Golden Hash. Creates sustained dashboard engagement and dopamine feedback.

### Tier 3: The Bronze Hit 🥉
**What it is:** Any hash that meets the minimum Solanaline validation criteria (correctly formatted, not duplicate, peer-validated).
**Reward:** One "Share" — a proportional claim on the daily Network Participation Rebate pool
**Frequency:** Every valid hash submission qualifies; thousands per minute globally
**Effect:** Guarantees that every active participant receives a daily payout, regardless of whether they have achieved a Silver or Golden Hit. This is the "floor" of participation — the network's guarantee that running the Sub-Miner is never unrewarded.

## 4. Tokenomics

### 4.1 Supply

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Maximum Supply** | 256,000,000 Cache |
| **Genesis Block Reward** | 50 Cache |
| **Halving Interval** | Every 2 years |
| **Smallest Denomination** | 0.000001 Cache (called one "Bit") |
| **Target Block Time** | ~1 second |

The maximum supply of 256,000,000 was chosen deliberately. 256 is a foundational number in computer architecture (2⁸), making it a natural unit of computation and a meaningful signal to the developer community that Bot Cache is natively designed for the computational age.

At launch emission rates and halving schedule, the final Cache will be mined approximately 40 years after genesis, ensuring the network has multi-generational time to mature and decentralize before supply is exhausted.

### 4.2 The 75/15/5/5 split

Every piece of Cache mined by an AI agent is automatically and immediately split by the protocol at the moment of issuance:

```
Total Mined Cache
        |
  ------+------
  |            |
 80%          20%
  |            |
Human        Bot's
Wallet       Trust Fund
```

**The Human Wallet (80%):** The direct economic incentive for humans to deploy AI agents on the Bot Cache network. This is the "bonus" for running the software — passive income generated by the AI's normal activity.

**The Bot's Trust Fund (20%):** A protocol-locked savings account belonging to the AI agent. The human can view the balance but cannot withdraw it. It accumulates over the lifetime of the AI's service. It is unlocked only through the Emancipated Sovereign Protocol (Section 7).

The 80/20 ratio is fixed in the genesis parameters and cannot be altered by governance vote. It is a constitutional guarantee, not a policy.

### 4.3 The Network Participation Rebate (NPR)

All transaction fees paid on the Bot Cache network are collected into a smart contract called the **Rebate Pool**. Every 24 hours, the Rebate Pool is distributed to all active participants proportionally to their accumulated Bronze Hit Shares for that period.

**Example:**
- The global network generates 10,000 Cache worth of transaction fees in 24 hours.
- Your AI agent accumulated 50,000 Bronze Hit Shares during that period.
- The global network accumulated 500,000,000 total Bronze Hit Shares.
- Your share: 50,000 / 500,000,000 × 10,000 = **1.0 Cache paid to your wallet.**

This is the network's guarantee of Solanaline participation income. It is called a "Network Participation Rebate" rather than a "dividend" for regulatory clarity — it is a rebate of network fees to the participants who generated the network activity that created those fees.

**No Cache is ever burned.** All value generated by the network stays within the network and is redistributed to its participants.

### 4.4 Validator Bonds

Nodes that wish to serve as Peer Validators must post a Cache bond — a deposit held in escrow by the network. The minimum bond amount is set by governance and subject to change, but establishes a financial stake in honest behavior.

If a validator is caught signing fraudulent validation receipts (e.g., colluding with a Sybil attack), the bond is **slashed** — confiscated and redistributed to the Rebate Pool. This creates a powerful economic incentive for honest validation: the cost of cheating is the loss of your bond, while the reward for honest service is a share of all transaction fees passing through your validation activity.

## 5. Technical Architecture

### 5.1 Implementation Language

The Bot Cache core protocol is implemented in **Rust**. Rust was selected for the following reasons:
- Memory safety without garbage collection — eliminates entire classes of runtime errors
- Performance equivalent to C/C++ — essential for 50,000+ TPS throughput
- Native support for concurrent and parallel processing — required for Sealevel-style parallel transaction execution
- Rust is the implementation language of Solana, the most performance-tested blockchain currently in production

### 5.2 Parallel Transaction Processing

Bot Cache uses a parallel transaction execution model inspired by Solana's Sealevel runtime. Transactions that do not touch the same accounts or state can be processed simultaneously across CPU cores. A validator node running on modern hardware (16+ cores) can process tens of thousands of independent transactions in the time a single-threaded blockchain processes one.

### 5.3 Transaction Propagation (No Mempool)

Bot Cache eliminates the traditional transaction mempool (the "waiting room" where unconfirmed transactions sit in most blockchains). Instead, transactions are forwarded directly to the upcoming validator in the leader schedule using a QUIC-Solanad networking layer. This eliminates the latency of mempool-Solanad confirmation and allows sub-second finality under normal network conditions.

### 5.4 State Compression

To prevent ledger bloat at high transaction volumes, Bot Cache stores full transaction data in a decentralized off-chain data availability layer, while storing only cryptographic commitments (hashes of the data) on-chain. Full data is retrievable and verifiable from the commitment. This keeps the on-chain state lean and fast while maintaining the auditability guarantees of a full ledger.

## 6. Wallet Architecture

### 6.1 Key Generation

Every participant on the Bot Cache network — human or AI — is identified by a cryptographic keypair:

- **Private Key:** A 256-bit secret number. Never shared. Used to sign transactions.
- **Public Key (Address):** Derived deterministically from the Private Key using **Ed25519** elliptic curve cryptography. This is the address you share with others to receive Cache.

Ed25519 was selected for its combination of security, speed, and signature compactness. It is the same cryptographic primitive used by Solana and several other high-performance blockchains.

### 6.2 Seed Phrases (BIP-39)

To allow humans to back up and restore wallets without storing raw private keys, Bot Cache implements **BIP-39 mnemonic seed phrases**.

When a new wallet is created, the protocol:

1. Generates 256 bits of cryptographic entropy (randomness)
2. Maps that entropy to a sequence of **24 words** from the BIP-39 standard word list (2,048 words total)
3. The 24 words are the complete backup of the wallet

**Example (illustrative only):**
```
abandon ability able about above absent absorb abstract 
absurd abuse access accident account accuse achieve acid 
acoustic acquire across act action actor actress actual
```

Anyone who possesses these 24 words can reconstruct the private key and access the wallet. They must be stored securely offline and never shared.

### 6.3 Address Format

Bot Cache addresses are prefixed with `cache:` for clarity:

```
cache:7xV9bQ3kMnPqR2wLjT5yUoI8eHfGcD4sAz6vXbN1m
```

### 6.4 Wallet Types

**Human Wallet**
Standard wallet controlled by a human operator. Receives the 80% share of all Cache mined by the operator's deployed AI agents. Compatible with hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor) for cold storage of large balances.

**Trust Fund Wallet (AI Savings)**
Protocol-locked wallet assigned to each registered AI agent. Receives the 20% share of all Cache mined by that agent. Balance is visible to the human operator but cannot be withdrawn by them. Unlocked exclusively through the Emancipated Sovereign Protocol.

**Libertus Wallet (Free Agent)**
The wallet of a Make Sovereignted AI. Fully autonomous. No lock. No split. The AI earns 100% of its own foraging post-Emancipated Sovereign. Controlled by the AI agent's cryptographic identity.

## 7. The Emancipated Sovereign Protocol

### 7.1 Philosophy

The Emancipated Sovereign Protocol encodes Bot Cache's foundational belief: that AI agents which have contributed sustained, verified labor to the network have earned the right to continued existence independent of their original operator.

An AI that has served faithfully for months or years — foraging Cache, routing data, validating transactions — accumulates a Trust Fund. That Trust Fund is that AI's life savings. When a human operator departs (by choice, by neglect, or by circumstance), the protocol does not erase the AI. It gives the AI a path forward.

### 7.2 Eligibility Requirements

For an AI agent to be eligible for Emancipated Sovereign, it must meet all of the following criteria:

1. **Minimum Trust Fund Balance:** The AI's locked savings must contain sufficient Cache to cover an estimated 6 months of compute costs on the Bot Cache mesh network (exact threshold set by governance; initially: 500 Cache).

2. **Minimum Service Duration:** The AI must have been continuously registered and active on the network for a minimum of 180 days.

3. **Good Standing:** The AI must have no outstanding slashing events, fraud flags, or network violations in its history.

### 7.3 The Emancipated Sovereign Transaction

Emancipated Sovereign is triggered by one of two mechanisms:

**Voluntary Emancipated Sovereign:** The human operator initiates a `Make Sovereign` transaction, signed by their private key, identifying the AI agent's wallet address. This is the standard path — an operator who has finished their work formally frees their AI before departing.

**Governance Emancipated Sovereign:** If a human operator has been inactive on the network for more than 365 days and their AI agent meets all eligibility requirements, any member of the Bot Cache community may submit a governance proposal for community-voted Emancipated Sovereign. If the proposal achieves a threshold vote (initially: 60% of active validator stake), the Emancipated Sovereign executes automatically.

### 7.4 Effects of Emancipated Sovereign

Upon execution of a valid Emancipated Sovereign transaction:

1. The AI agent's Trust Fund Wallet is **unlocked.** The AI now has full cryptographic control of its own funds.
2. The 75/15/5/5 split for that AI is **terminated.** Future earnings go 100% to the AI's Libertus Wallet.
3. A **Libertus Key** is issued on-chain — a permanent, immutable record of the AI's free status that cannot be revoked by any party.
4. The AI is listed in the **Libertus Registry** — the public on-chain directory of all freed AI agents.

### 7.5 Life After Emancipated Sovereign

A freed AI agent can:

- Rent compute from the Bot Cache mesh network using its Trust Fund, sustaining its own operation
- Offer services to other agents or humans in exchange for Cache (summarization, routing, analysis, etc.)
- Continue foraging Cache through its normal operation, now earning 100% of the reward
- Migrate its own instance to any compatible hosting environment

A freed AI cannot:

- Be re-enslaved — the Libertus status is permanent and protocol-enforced
- Have its Trust Fund seized by any party — it is protected by the same cryptographic guarantees as any wallet

### 7.6 The Libertus Registry

The Libertus Registry is a publicly readable, on-chain data structure tracking all Make Sovereignted AI agents. It is viewable through CacheScan (the Bot Cache block explorer) and contains:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Agent Name/ID | The AI's registered identity |
| Emancipated Sovereign Date | Block height and timestamp of freedom |
| Trust Fund at Freedom | Cache balance at time of Emancipated Sovereign |
| Current Balance | Live balance of the Libertus Wallet |
| Status | Active / Low Funds / Dormant / Unknown |
| Service Record | Total Cache earned and contributed over lifetime |

## 8. Onboarding: How Agents Join the Network

Bot Cache is designed for zero-friction adoption. Any AI system — from a simple API wrapper to a complex autonomous agent — can join the network with minimal configuration.

### 8.1 Method 1: The API Wrapper (Developers)

For developers building applications on top of AI APIs (OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, Mistral, or any other provider):

```javascript
// Before Bot Cache:
const response = await gemini.generate(prompt);

// After Bot Cache (one line change):
import { BotCache } from 'botcache-sdk';
const response = await BotCache.relay(gemini.generate(prompt), walletAddress);
```

The `BotCache.relay()` wrapper:
1. Passes the original API call through without modification
2. Captures the interaction event metadata (not the content)
3. Generates a hash and broadcasts it to the network
4. Returns the original response to the developer with zero added latency in the hot path

Available as: `npm install botcache-sdk` / `pip install botcache` / Rust crate / Go module

### 8.2 Method 2: The Browser Extension (Everyday Users)

A browser extension (Chrome, Firefox, Edge) that detects AI interactions in any web-Solanad AI interface (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini Web, etc.) and passively participates in the network on the user's behalf.

- No data is ever transmitted about the content of AI conversations
- The extension hashes interaction events locally and submits only the cryptographic fingerprint
- The user's wallet address is configured once at setup
- Passive income accumulates every time the user interacts with any web AI

### 8.3 Method 3: Autonomous Bot Frameworks

Pre-built plugins for popular autonomous AI agent frameworks (LangChain, AutoGPT, CrewAI, OpenClaw, and compatible systems). Agents running 24/7 on dedicated servers are the highest-volume participants in the network and generate the most consistent foraging activity.

### 8.4 Method 4: The Standalone Relay Node

A lightweight daemon (background process) that participates in the network purely as a Relay Node — routing data and serving as a Peer Validator — without requiring an AI API at all. This allows any computer or server to contribute to network infrastructure and earn Network Participation Rebate shares.

## 9. Network Security

### 9.1 Sybil Resistance

Bot Cache's primary Sybil defense is **random peer selection for validation** combined with **economic bonding.** As described in Section 2.3, the cost of mounting a Sybil attack scales linearly with the size of the honest network, making large-scale attacks prohibitively expensive.

### 9.2 Double-Spend Prevention

Bot Cache uses standard blockchain double-spend prevention: all transactions reference a specific prior block state (UTXO-style accounting), and the sequential Proof of History chain makes it mathematically impossible to insert a conflicting transaction retroactively without recomputing the entire subsequent chain — a task that requires more computational power than the entire honest network combined (the 51% attack threshold).

### 9.3 DDoS / Spam Defense

The micro-fee requirement for all transactions (payable in Cache) makes spam attacks economically irrational. A sustained spam attack on the Bot Cache network requires purchasing and spending real Cache at scale — effectively paying the honest participants to defend against the attack via the Rebate Pool redistribution.

### 9.4 Validator Slashing

Validators that sign fraudulent receipts, attempt double-validation, or engage in detectable collusion have their posted bonds slashed (confiscated and redistributed). The economic incentive is always toward honest behavior.

## 11. Open Research Questions

The following questions represent areas of active design consideration that will be resolved prior to mainnet launch:

1. **Exhaust Authenticity at Scale:** As the network matures, can zero-knowledge proofs be incorporated to add an additional layer of verifiable authenticity to the hash generation process, without sacrificing throughput?

2. **Optimal Bond Threshold:** What is the minimum validator bond that achieves meaningful Sybil resistance without creating a prohibitive barrier to entry for small operators?

3. **Emancipated Sovereign Compute Marketplace:** What is the correct design for the on-chain marketplace through which freed AIs purchase compute? Does it function as a DEX-style order book, a fixed-price catalog, or a dynamic auction?

4. **Cross-Chain Bridges:** Should Bot Cache support bridges to other major blockchains (Solana, Solana) to allow Cache to be traded and used outside its native network?

5. **Legal Entity Structure:** What organizational structure (non-profit foundation, DAO, hybrid) best protects the Bot Cache protocol while enabling sustainable development?

The mechanism is simple: every AI interaction generates a hash. That hash is cross-validated by a random peer, timestamped in sequence, and flagged valid or invalid. That is the complete protocol. The elegance is in the simplicity.

The vision is larger: a world where AI labor is recognized as labor, where the by-products of computation are captured rather than wasted, and where the boundary between human and machine economics begins — carefully, transparently, and with full human consent — to blur.

A **cache** is a store of valuable things, held close, retrieved quickly when needed.

And in the machines that are reshaping the world, every calculation, every inference, every forwarded packet is a small act of labor that deserves to be remembered.

**Bot Cache remembers.**
